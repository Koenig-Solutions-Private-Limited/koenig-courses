## What You Will Learn
- Master CAD software like Fusion 360 or SolidWorks for creating printable 3D models optimized for prototyping
- Configure slicer tools such as Cura or PrusaSlicer to generate G-code with custom supports and infill patterns
- Operate FDM and resin-based printers, including bed leveling, nozzle maintenance, and multi-material setups
- Select and test filaments or resins based on mechanical properties like tensile strength and thermal resistance
- Apply finishing techniques including sanding, vapor smoothing, and assembly for functional prototypes

## Who Should Attend
This course suits mechanical engineers, product designers, industrial drafters, and entry-to-mid-level makers with basic CAD familiarity. IT hardware developers or hobbyists aiming for professional prototyping roles will benefit, especially those with 1-3 years in design or fabrication.
